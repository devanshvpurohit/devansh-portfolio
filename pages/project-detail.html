Low-Latency Motor Controller

Architecting real-time feedback loops for high-torque BLDC motors.

Lead Engineer

3 Months

C++20, STM32

The Problem

Off-the-shelf individual motor controllers lacked the deterministic timing required for multi-axis synchronous movement in robotic arms. High latency in the feedback loop caused oscillation and instability during high-speed operations.

The Approach

I implemented a custom Field Oriented Control (FOC) algorithm using Modern C++. By leveraging hardware interrupts and DMA channels on the STM32, I was able to reduce the control loop latency to under 50 microseconds.

Impact & Results

The final controller yielded a 40% reduction in peak power consumption and enabled precision movements at speeds 3x faster than previous iterations. The project is now being used as the core driver in several experimental robotic platforms.
